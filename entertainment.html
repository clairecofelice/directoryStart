See & Do

  • The Gateway Arch

    • At 630 feet, the Gateway Arch is the world's tallest arch, the tallest man-made monument in the Western Hemisphere, and the state of Missouri's tallest accessible building. Construction on the arch began in the 1930s as a means of commemorating the United State's westward expansion. Due to its historical and architectural merits, the Arch attracts thousands of visitors each year and exists as one of the key symbols of St. Louis.

  • Busch Stadium

    • Busch Stadium is a baseball park located in the heart of the city and home to the St. Louis Cardinals. Each season, Busch Stadium hosts games attended by millions of baseball fans. Between 2006 and 2015, the Cardinals were ranked 2nd in their league for four years and 3rd for another five seasons. Whether you're a baseball lover or are simply looking for good snacks and good company, Busch Stadium is a must-see stop on any visitor to St. Louis' itinerary.

  • The City Museum

    • The City Museum in St. Louis' sprawling Forest Park is not your average museum. Built from repurposed architectural and industrial objects, the City Museum is known as an "eclectic mixture of children's playground, funhouse, surrealist pavilon, and architectural marvel." The museum offers children and adults alike an entirely interactive experience, encouraging visitors to explore all corner's of the facility without inhibitions.
